Katherine Johnson

NASA Hero & Mathematician

Katherine Johnson seated at her desk before computing the trajectory for Alan Shephard's space flight.

Here's a time line of Katherine Johnson's life:

  • 1918 - born as Creola Katherine Coleman on August 26, 1918, in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia
  • 1933 - From a young age, Katherine showed strong math and science abilities and graduated school at 14 years old and then enrolled at West Virginia State College
  • 1937 - Graduated Summa Cum Laude with degrees in mathematics and French at age 18
  • 1939 - Enrolled in a graduate math program at West Virginia University and became the first African American woman to attend this school.
  • 1953 - Accepted a job at the Langley Memorial Aeronautical Laboratory, based in Hampton, Virginia after working towards a career in science and mathematics.
  • 1953-1958 - worked as a computer, analyzing topics such as gust alleviation for aircrafts; originally assigned to the West Area Computers section supervised by mathematician Dorothy Vaughan; then Johnson was reassigned to the Guidance and Control Division of Langley's Flight Research Division
  • 1958 - worked as an aerospace technologist, moving during her career to the Spacecraft Controls Branch
  • 1961 - calculated the trajectory for the May 5, 1961 space flight of Alan Shepard, the first American in space; she also calculated the launch window for his 1961 Mercury mission
  • 1969 - helped calculate the trajectory for the 1969 Apollo 11 flight to the Moon
  • 1970 - worked on the Apollo 13 Moon mission; when the mission was aborted, her work on backup procedures and charts helped set a safe path for the crew's return to Earth
  • 1977 - received the NASA Group Achievement Award presented to the Lunar Spacecraft and Operations team – for pioneering work in the field of navigation supporting the spacecraft that orbited and mapped the Moon in preparation for the Apollo program
  • 1986 - retired from NASA
  • 2015 - awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom which was presented by President Barack Obama
  • 2020 - passed away on February 24, 2020, at age 101

“Even as a professional in an integrated world, I had been the only black woman in enough drawing rooms and boardrooms to have an inkling of the chutzpah it took for an African American woman in a segregated southern workplace to tell her bosses she was sure her calculations would put a man on the Moon.”

-- Katherine Johnson
